PipeCrawler

Designed new iteration and built prototype for cutting-edge pipe-inspection robot

I began by designing the roll-coupling mechanism. I first designed a sturdy actuator connection for the Dynamixel PH54-200-S500-R. Then I designed a variable angle joint for adjustability and a more robust design--the locking adjustable angle would allow for implementation in different pipe diameters. It was imperative that the roll-coupling remain in the middle axis of the pipe.

Next, I redesigned the omniwheel modules to make them more compact and shorter. This important for the robot to be able to navigate sharper turns. I redesigned the components with compact wire channels and a sturdy organic connection.

While designing the modules and planning the carbon-fiber tube connection lengths between the robot's modues, I wrote functions for the angle and connection length variables, and used a Python mathematical simulation tool (developed specifically for this project) to model the geometry of the robot's navigation through various pipe turns.

Then, I moved on to the prototyping phase. I 3D-printed all of the components, and assembled a prototype of this iteration of the robot. I used a special technique to cut the hexagonal carbon-fiber tubes that connect each of the robot's modules, and used heat inserts for all of the bolted connection between plastic parts.
